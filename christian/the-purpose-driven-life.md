# The Purpose Driven Life

**by Rick Warren (2002)**

## Author Background

Rick Warren (born January 28, 1954) is an American evangelical Christian pastor and author. He was born in San Jose, California, to Jimmy Warren, a Baptist minister, and Dot Warren, a high-school librarian. Raised in Ukiah, California, Warren graduated from Ukiah High School in 1972, where he notably founded the first Christian club on the school's campus.

Warren earned a Bachelor of Arts from California Baptist University, followed by a Master of Divinity from Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary in 1979, and later a Doctor of Ministry from Fuller Theological Seminary. In 1980, he founded Saddleback Church in Orange County, California, which grew into an evangelical megachurch averaging nearly 20,000 attendees weekly. Since 2022, he serves as executive director of the Finishing the Task mission coalition.

Warren has been recognized as one of Time magazine's "100 Most Influential People in the World" in 2005 and has spoken at major international forums including the United Nations, the World Economic Forum, and TED.

## Book Summary

*The Purpose Driven Life* is a Bible study book written by Christian pastor Rick Warren and published by Zondervan in 2002. The book is structured as a 40-day personal spiritual journey, with each short chapter designed to be read on consecutive days. It presents what Warren describes as God's five purposes for human life on Earth.

Warren frames the book as an "anti-self-help book," emphasizing that personal fulfillment, satisfaction, and meaning can only be found in understanding and doing what God placed each person on Earth to do. The first sentence of the book famously reads, "It's not about you," setting the tone for a theologically-driven approach to purpose and meaning.

The book has had remarkable cultural impact, influencing everyone from heads of state (including Rwanda's President Paul Kagame) to Olympic athletes (Michael Phelps read it during rehab). It has been translated into numerous languages and used as a tool in prisons, churches, and recovery programs worldwide.

## Theological Analysis

*The Purpose Driven Life* represents a contemporary evangelical approach to Christian spirituality that emphasizes practical application and church-centered purpose. Warren's theology draws heavily on:

- **The Great Commandment** (Matthew 22:37-40): Loving God and loving others
- **The Great Commission** (Matthew 28:19-20): Making disciples of all nations
- **Soteriology**: A clear presentation of salvation through Christ's death and resurrection
- **Ecclesiology**: Strong emphasis on the local church as the context for spiritual growth
- **Eschatology**: Brief references to the mission of God in history

The book has been criticized by some biblical scholars, including Robert M. Price, for applying eisegesis (reading meaning into Scripture) and selectively choosing Bible translations that support Warren's theses. Others have noted that scripture citations appear only in end notes, making it harder for readers to verify the biblical context.

Despite these critiques, the book has resonated with millions of readers for its accessible, practical approach to Christian living and its focus on moving beyond personal spirituality to active engagement in God's purposes.

## Key Takeaways

1. **It's Not About You** - True fulfillment comes from living for God's purposes, not personal achievement
2. **Five Core Purposes** - Worship, Fellowship, Discipleship, Ministry, and Mission define the Christian life
3. **Worship is Paramount** - We exist to bring pleasure to God through our lives
4. **Community is Essential** - Christians are meant to grow in the context of Christian fellowship
5. **Discipleship Requires Transformation** - Becoming like Christ is a lifelong process
6. **Service is Non-Negotiable** - Every believer has a role to play in ministry
7. **Mission is Mandatory** - Sharing the gospel is part of following Jesus

## Overall Assessment

*The Purpose Driven Life* is arguably one of the most influential Christian books of the 21st century. Its accessible writing style and practical 40-day format have made it readable for millions who might not pick up a traditional theology book.

**Strengths:**
- Highly accessible and readable
- Practical application focus
- Comprehensive view of Christian purpose
- Has motivated genuine spiritual growth for many readers
- Strong emphasis on the local church

**Weaknesses:**
- Some theological oversimplification
- Critiqued for eisegetical interpretation of Scripture
- Can feel formulaic at times
- Limited depth for theological nuance

**Conclusion:** Whether readers agree with every theological nuance or not, *The Purpose Driven Life* has fundamentally shaped contemporary evangelical spirituality and church practice. Its influence on millions lives worldwide—through personal spiritual formation, church planting movements, and humanitarian initiatives—makes it a significant work in modern Christian literature.
